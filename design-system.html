Atoms
Buttons
Four variants covering the full action hierarchy. Always use the lowest-weight variant that still communicates the importance of the action.
Variants
Primary — main call-to-action per view
Secondary — supporting actions, equal to "Cancel"
Ghost — low-emphasis, inline actions
Danger outline — destructive actions
Validate CTA — full-width, primary action within activity card
Reopen — tinted green, used on validated activities
DoOne Primary button per screen region. Pair it with a Secondary or Ghost for cancel/back. The Validate CTA is only used inside expanded activity cards.
Don'tUse Primary for every action — it loses hierarchy. Don't use Danger Outline for confirmation steps; reserve it for genuinely destructive, irreversible actions.

Atoms
Chips
Two chip types: role chips for team context in alignment cards; engagement chips for recording who was consulted during an activity.
Role chips
Neutral for most roles; brand-tinted for Stakeholders to indicate external involvement.
Designer Engineer PM Researcher Business Stakeholder
Engagement chips
Used inside activity cards to record who was engaged during the work. Inactive chips are toggled on with a single click; active chips show the team name, an optional date, and a remove button.
Inactive — not yet engaged
Active — engaged, with and without timestamp
✓ Stakeholders
✓ PM team
DoLog dates whenever the engagement was a formal review or sign-off moment — it creates a lightweight audit trail.
Don'tPre-populate engagement chips — they must be set intentionally by the user to be meaningful.

Alignment Card
Used in the project and phase views to surface rule-based alignment prompts. Two variants: Required (left border accent, amber tint) and Consider (neutral).
⚠ Required
Business stakeholders must review capability prioritisation

Your project includes business stakeholders. Outcome prioritisation decisions need their sign-off before moving to object modelling.

↗ Consider
Engineering should be present for entity discovery

Entity naming has direct implications for data modelling. Including engineering early prevents expensive rework later.
